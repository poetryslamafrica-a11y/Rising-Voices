What We're Building

The Mission

The Rising Voices Schools' Slam is Kenya's first nationwide high school spoken word and slam poetry competition — a platform that doesn't just run a contest, but builds a movement.

It begins months before any stage: with workshops in classrooms, poetry clubs in schools, and mentorship from practicing slam poets. By the time students reach the competition, they have been trained, rehearsed, and supported — not just entered.

The program runs on a two-year calendar. 2026 is the incubation and qualifying year — five regional heats across Kenya, a national semi-final, and a national final in January 2027 at Poetry Slam Africa Festival. 2027 extends the season, deepens the incubation, and builds toward a mature national circuit by 2028.

Curated by Creative Spills, this competition serves as a conduit for education, cultural preservation, and the promotion of a vibrant poetry culture across all 47 counties.

Program Objectives
  • 01 //Raise awareness of slam poetry as an autonomous art form in Kenyan schools
  • 02 //Build confidence, self-esteem, and public speaking skills in young people
  • 03 //Promote gender equality and inclusion through creative expression
  • 04 //Foster cultural integration and national cohesion across regions
  • 05 //Establish poetry clubs and community hubs in learning institutions
  • 06 //Develop a Pan-African oral art culture with a vibrant poetry scene across all counties by 2030
The Incubation Program

Three phases.
One movement.

Phase 01 // Outreach
School Outreach & Spoken Word Training
Foundation — building the base

Before any competition, we go into schools. This phase is about planting seeds — training teachers, establishing poetry clubs, and giving students their first real encounter with spoken word as a living art form.

  • Virtual and in-school info sessions for teachers and students
  • Spoken word fundamentals workshops (voice, breath, body, presence)
  • Introduction to African slam culture and its history
  • Establishing School Poetry Ambassadors per institution
  • Distribution of the Creative Spills Schools Toolkit
  • Online mentorship sessions with professional slam poets
  • School registration and team formation support
Feb – April 2026  ·  All Regions
Phase 02 // Slam Training
Slam Technique & Competition Preparation
Performance — getting stage-ready

The craft deepens. Students who showed interest in Phase 01 enter intensive preparation — learning what distinguishes slam from recitation, how to write for competition, and how to perform under pressure.

  • Slam rules, scoring structure, and competition etiquette
  • Writing workshops: drafting and editing original slam poems
  • Performance coaching: delivery, timing, emotional truth
  • Peer slam practice rounds within schools
  • Regional pre-slam sessions: informal performances before heats
  • Group and duo poem development for team categories
  • Digital submission of qualifying poems for judging shortlist
April – May 2026  ·  Pre-Heat Period
Phase 03 // Competition
Regional Qualifiers & National Slam
Championship — the full arc

Five regional heats across Kenya, culminating in a national semi-final and the Rising Voices National Schools Championship at Poetry Slam Africa 2027. The full competitive journey from classroom to national stage.

  • 5 regional heats (Nairobi, Coast, Western, Rift Valley, Central/Eastern)
  • 8–12 schools per heat, top 2 competitors advance
  • National Semi-Final: November 2026, Nairobi
  • National Schools Championship: 29 January 2027
  • Professional judging panel of 5 per event
  • Trophy, medals, certificates, vouchers, and cash prizes
  • Champion receives mentorship year and 2028 performance slot
May – November 2026  ·  Heats + Finals Jan 2027
Training Content

What students
actually learn.

Spoken Word Foundations
  • Voice & Breath Session 1 · 90 min

    Diaphragmatic breathing, projection, resonance. Learning to fill a room without a microphone and to use silence as a performance tool.

  • Body & Presence Session 2 · 90 min

    Stage movement, eye contact, the use of gesture and stillness. How the body tells the story the words begin.

  • Writing for the Stage Session 3–4 · 2×90 min

    Oral poetry is written differently from page poetry. Rhythm, sound, repetition, and the line break as breath — the craft of words made to be heard.

  • African Oral Traditions Session 5 · 60 min

    The griot tradition, praise poetry, ijala, taarab, and modern African slam. Where we come from shapes how we perform.

  • Identity & Authentic Voice Session 6 · 90 min

    Finding personal story. Writing from lived experience. Confronting the blank page with something true to say.

Slam Competition Skills
  • Slam Rules & Format Session 7 · 60 min

    How scoring works. What judges look for. The 3-minute rule. Props, costumes, and what disqualifies a performance. Knowing the field before you take it.

  • Competitive Writing Session 8–9 · 2×90 min

    Writing a poem that can survive a slam: balancing artistic integrity with the awareness that five strangers will score you. Editing, refining, rehearsing.

  • Mock Slam Rounds Session 10 · 120 min

    Full simulation of a slam event with audience, judges, and scores. The pressure is real; the environment is safe. Building performance muscle memory.

  • Group & Duo Poems Session 11 · 90 min

    Collaborative writing and performance. Call-and-response, layered voices, synchronized movement. The additional discipline of making two or three voices sound like one intention.

  • Pre-Heat Masterclass Session 12 · 120 min

    Final polish session with a professional slam poet. One-on-one feedback on competition pieces. Mental preparation, stage nerves, the ritual of performing.

Season Two

2027
Season

The second season deepens everything the first built. More schools, stronger incubation, a longer training window, and a competition structure that has been tested and refined by a full year of running.

By 2027, the Rising Voices Schools' Slam is no longer a first edition — it is an institution. Schools come in with returning Poetry Ambassadors, established clubs, and alumni from the 2026 season coaching the next generation.

The 2027 national champion performs at Poetry Slam Africa 2028 and represents Kenya at an African regional slam competition — the first step toward a Pan-African schools circuit.
